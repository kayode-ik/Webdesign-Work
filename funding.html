Funding

Herconomy, a platform that aims to economically empower women, has just completed a successful pre-seed round of £450,000 in just 24 hours, showcasing the growing investor appetite for female equality.
Based in Lagos, Nigeria, the organisation counts 15,000 entrepreneurs and professionals from around the world as members, including 1,500 UK members. A significant portion of their latest funding round came from its UK-based committee members and angel investors.
Herconomy provides female members with training, jobs and capacity building opportunities. They also offer access to grants, loans, scholarships, employment and investment and has partnered with both global and local brands to provide women with discounts on key products and services.

Use of funds and customer innovation The funds will be used to improve recruitment, marketing and technology capacities and help Herconomy become the world’s leading neobank for women, according to its female founder, Ife Durosinmi-Etti.
They also want to further develop UK partnerships to support women and build closer business links between UK female-focused companies and the Nigerian business community, enabling the creation of a more diverse and inclusive transnational business ecosystem.
Herconomy’s aim to become a leading female-focused neobank, also known as fully digital platforms that support customers with their financial needs, is part of the financial technology revolution and is innovating the customer experience compared to what traditional banks can offer.
This means Herconomy’s digital services have the potential to reach a more diverse group of women and economically empower them on a global scale.
